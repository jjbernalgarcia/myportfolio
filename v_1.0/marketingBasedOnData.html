Data-driven marketing

"If you don’t know what a customer is worth, you don’t know what you should spend to acquire a new one or keep one"

What could I add to the strategy of your company?


Implement a process of rapid experimentation across marketing channels and product development to identify the most efficient and profitable ways to grow the business.
Measure and optimize the Customer Lifetime Value (LTV). LTV is the revenue that you acquire from a customer during their lifetime. Customers do not represent single transactions, but an ongoing relationship.
Optimize revenue and budget.
Define Cohorts and Segmentation Channels to run optimization experimentation.
Determine the allowable Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) you should budget across channels.

KPI we must measure and optimize

Term Meaning Why is Important? Example
Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) The value (revenue) that a customer accrues over their lifetime LTV dictates how much you can spend acquiring new customers “Our business’s customers have a LTV of $250”
Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) The cost of acquiring a new customer How much are you actually spending? “We currently have a CAC of $55, but we want to get it down to $40”
Click-ThroughRate The % of people that click something, generally an email Growth marketers want to increase this number “Our current CTR is only 14% on re-engagement campaigs”
Conversion Generally a %, how many people that visit purchase or buy? Conversion is one of the most important metrics to increase “Currently 22% of visitors to our website convert a booking”
Return on Ad Spend How much revenue do you get for each dollar spent? When comparing marketing campaigns, optimize for ROAS “Facebook is high volume, but it’s ROAS is 1.21 vs. 1.8 for Twitter”
